About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at MIT department of Brain & Cognitive Sciences. Previously, I was a PhD student in the College of Engineering and Computer Science at the Australian National University, advised by Prof. Richard Hartley. I received my bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Tehran. My research interests lie in the fields of Computer Vision and Machine Learning. Specifically, I work on problems with limited data/supervision, data diversity design for out-of-distribution generalization (systematic and syntax), deep neural network confidence calibration, and inference in probabilistic graphical models. I am also interested in meta-learning and deep invertible networks.
